Comparatives of inferiority in English are used to express that something is "less" than something else. There are two main ways to form them:

1. With "less":

Used for adjectives that are not countable and don't have a plural way.

  • Example: Beautiful → Less beautiful (menos hermoso)
  • Example: Interesting → Less interesting (menos interesante)

2. With "fewer":

Used for countable nouns and do have a plural way.

  • Example: Books → Fewer books (menos libros)
  • Example: Cars → Fewer cars (menos carros)

Comparatives of Equality

Comparatives of equality in English are used to express that two things are the same, that is, that they have the same quality or characteristic. There are two main ways to form them:

1. "As...as" (for adjectives/adverbs)

This is the most common structure to express equality. It means that one thing is as (adjective/adverb) as another.

Structure: As + adjective/adverb + as (tan + adjective/adverb + como)

Example: Tall → As tall as (tan alto como)

John is as tall as Paul. (John es tan alto como Paul.)
